More on Product Information

VITOR Tablets contain Methoxsalen, a photosensitizing agent used along with UVA therapy for managing conditions like psoriasis and vitiligo. This treatment is known as PUVA (Psoralen + UVA) therapy. Methoxsalen increases skin sensitivity to UV light, promoting melanocyte stimulation and pigmentation in affected areas.

Always follow the treatment plan provided by your dermatologist, which includes correct dosing, timing of UVA exposure, and protective measures like eye protection and limiting sun exposure outside of controlled therapy sessions.

FAQ

Q: How should I take VITOR Tablets?

A: Take as prescribed by your doctor, usually a specified time before UVA therapy. Do not change the schedule without medical advice.

Q: Can I go out in the sun after taking this?

A: Avoid direct sunlight after taking Methoxsalen, as it can increase sensitivity and risk of sunburn. Follow your doctor’s guidelines on sun exposure.

Q: What are the possible side effects?

A: Side effects may include redness, itching, or mild discomfort. Long-term use requires monitoring for potential skin and eye changes. Consult your doctor if you experience unusual symptoms.

Q: Is it safe during pregnancy?

A: Consult your doctor if you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant. The decision will depend on potential risks and benefits.
